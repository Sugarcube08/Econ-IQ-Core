# Feature Store & AI Readiness Audit

This document audits the analytical features generated by the Polars feature engineering pipelines in the econiq codebase and evaluates their readiness for training predictive Machine Learning models.

---

## 1. Inventory of Generated Features

The pipeline generates the following rolling features in a 365-day historical window (and a 14-day recent window):

| Feature Name | Source Module | Mathematical Formulation | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| `sales_window` | `FeatureEngineer` | $\sum \text{sales}$ in 365d window | Total purchase volume scale. |
| `payments_window` | `FeatureEngineer` | $\sum \text{payments}$ in 365d window | Total cash cleared. |
| `penalty_window` | `FeatureEngineer` | $\sum \text{returned goods value}$ in 365d | Raw returned goods burden. |
| `events_window` | `FeatureEngineer` | $\text{count}(\text{events})$ in 365d | Interaction frequency. |
| `participation_density` | `FeatureEngineer` | $\text{purchase\_days} / \text{active\_duration\_days}$ | Trading consistency (percentage of active days with orders). |
| `log_sales_scale` | `FeatureEngineer` | $\log_{10}(\text{sales\_window} + 1.0)$ | Scale-invariant volume metric. |
| `last_purchased_at` | `FeatureEngineer` | Cumulative Max of Sale Date | Recency anchor (days since last order). |
| `debit_persistence_days`| `LedgerReconstruction` | Cumulative sum of consecutive days outstanding $> 0$ | Duration of continuous indebtedness. |
| `avg_repayment_days` | `SettlementMatching` | Median/mean delay between sales and matching receipts | Velocity of debt clearance. |
| `overdue_120p_amount` | `SettlementMatching` | $\sum \text{outstanding balances aged } > 120\text{d}$ | High-risk aged default amount. |
| `repayment_regularity_score`| `PaymentRhythm` | Coefficient of Variation of repayment gap days | Volatility of repayment behavior. |
| `repayment_fragmentation` | `PaymentRhythm` | $\text{receipt\_count} / \text{matching\_sales\_count}$ | Habit of paying in partial, fragmented chunks. |
| `exposure_pressure_score` | `ExposurePressure` | Ratio of outstanding to rolling historical sales scale | Leverage / burden score. |
| `trade_regularity_score` | `TradeConsistency` | Median deviation of order cadence | Purchasing pattern consistency. |

## 2. AI Use Case Feasibility Matrix

We evaluate if these features are sufficient to build the five target predictive modules for the Econiq Hackathon:

### 2.1 Risk Prediction (Probability of Default)
*   **Feasibility Status:** **Ready (95%)**
*   **Ready Features:** `avg_repayment_days`, `overdue_120p_amount`, `exposure_pressure_score`, `debit_persistence_days`, `repayment_regularity_score`, `stress_score`.
*   **Missing Requirements:** Credit bureau history (optional for hackathon, critical for production); macro-economic regional defaults.
*   **Explainability Driver:** Delinquency aging (`overdue_120p_amount`) and slow repayment times (`avg_repayment_days`) are the top drivers.

### 2.2 Growth Prediction (Trading Potential)
*   **Feasibility Status:** **Partially Ready (75%)**
*   **Ready Features:** `log_sales_scale`, `participation_density`, `trade_regularity_score`, `velocity_ratio`.
*   **Missing Requirements:** Industry vertical trend indicators, region-level commerce health indexes.
*   **Explainability Driver:** Rising velocity ratios (`velocity_ratio > 1.2`) and high participation density.

### 2.3 Health Prediction (Business Operations)
*   **Feasibility Status:** **Ready (90%)**
*   **Ready Features:** `rg_rate_score`, `repayment_regularity_score`, `cadence_class`, `stress_score`.
*   **Missing Requirements:** Distributor inventory turn cycles; client margin indicators.
*   **Explainability Driver:** Elevated return ratios (`rg_rate_score > 0.3`) indicating supply friction.

### 2.4 Churn Prediction (Merchant Attrition)
*   **Feasibility Status:** **Ready (85%)**
*   **Ready Features:** `last_purchased_at` (recency), `active_duration_days` (tenure), `events_recent` vs `events_window` (drop in frequency).
*   **Missing Requirements:** Merchant profile edits or portal login inactivity logs.
*   **Explainability Driver:** Days since last purchase (recency $> 60$ days) combined with a collapsing velocity ratio ($< 0.3$).

### 2.5 Collection Prioritization (Delinquency Recovery)
*   **Feasibility Status:** **Ready (90%)**
*   **Ready Features:** `outstanding_amount`, `overdue_120p_amount`, `repayment_fragmentation`, `debit_persistence_days`, `avg_repayment_days`.
*   **Missing Requirements:** Historical collection action logs (calls made, promises to pay).
*   **Explainability Driver:** High outstanding balances coupled with high payment fragmentation (indicates cash flow strain).

## 3. Future Deep Learning (DL) Feature Strategy

To prepare for future sequential deep learning models (e.g. Transformers or LSTMs for cash flow forecasting):
*   **Required Data Structure:** Sequence of daily feature vectors (matrices of shape `[batch, sequence_length, features]`) rather than static window rollups.
*   **Action Plan:** The Polars engineering pipeline must be modified to output daily chronological arrays for each customer, preserving the precise temporal sequence of events.
